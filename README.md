# Alignment-of-Business-and-IT

## Overview

This repository documents the academic work completed for the course **Alignment of Business and IT (ABIT)** .

The project focuses on understanding how organizations align business strategies, business processes, and information technology systems to support organizational objectives and improve operational efficiency. The assignment explores how strategic frameworks, business modeling, and digital technologies contribute to effective coordination between business and IT functions.


## Academic Learning

Through this project, I developed a deeper understanding of how organizations integrate **business strategy and IT capabilities** to achieve strategic objectives. The assignment emphasized analyzing business processes, understanding value creation mechanisms, and examining how digital technologies support organizational operations.

The project also demonstrated how structured frameworks and modeling techniques can be used to analyze business environments, support decision-making, and improve coordination between business functions and IT systems.


## Key Concepts Learned

### Business–IT Alignment
Business–IT alignment focuses on ensuring that information technology supports business strategies and organizational objectives. Effective alignment allows organizations to improve operational efficiency, support innovation, and maintain competitive advantages.

### Enterprise Architecture
Enterprise Architecture (EA) provides a structured approach for aligning business processes, information systems, and technology infrastructure. It helps organizations design and manage their IT landscape in a way that supports business strategies and long-term organizational goals. Enterprise architecture enables organizations to understand the relationships between business capabilities, applications, and IT infrastructure.

### Business Process Modeling
Business processes were analyzed and modeled using **Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN)** to visualize workflows, activities, and decision points. Process modeling helps organizations understand operations, identify inefficiencies, and improve business processes.

### Value Proposition
The **Value Proposition** describes how an organization creates value for its customers by addressing their needs and solving specific problems. Understanding the value proposition helps connect business strategies with customer expectations and market demands.

### Business Model Canvas
The **Business Model Canvas** was used to analyze how organizations create, deliver, and capture value. The framework includes key elements such as customer segments, value propositions, key activities, key resources, partnerships, and revenue streams.
